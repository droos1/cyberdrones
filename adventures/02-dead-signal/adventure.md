# Adventure 2: Dead Signal

## Background

The players destroyed the primary transmitter on level 17, but the droneware is still embedded in their nervous systems. Lazard has a mobile backup transmitter — lower range, but fully functional. He's dispatched a recovery team to locate the players, reactivate the droneware, and eliminate them as evidence.

Meanwhile, **The Mole** has gone deeper underground inside Arasaka. The internal investigation into level 17 is creating chaos, and Lazard is burning evidence fast. The Mole knows that the backup transmitter is being calibrated and triangulating the players' signal. Once the recovery team is close enough — it's over.

The players need to find a ripperdoc capable of extracting military-grade Arasaka cyberware from their nervous systems. The only one in Night City who can do it needs something in return.

**Core tension:** Get the droneware out before Arasaka gets close enough to flip the switch.

## Adventure Structure

### Part 1: Fire Escape (Cold Open)

Picks up directly from Adventure 1. The players are on the fire escape of the Arasaka building, level 17 is in chaos behind them — alarms, fire suppression, smoke pouring from windows.

**The Descent:**
- Fire escape is old, rusted, shaking. ATHLETICS/DEX check (DV 13) or take 1d6 damage from a collapsing section
- Below: emergency vehicles arriving, corporate security redirecting to the building entrance
- AV-4 spotlight sweeps the building exterior — STEALTH check (DV 15) to avoid being lit up
- If spotted: AV circles back, door gunner opens up. Suppressive fire (4d6), players need to get to ground level fast

**Street Level:**
- Crowd chaos. Evacuees, onlookers, corpo security shouting. Perfect cover.
- STEALTH (DV 11) to blend in and walk away, or ATHLETICS (DV 13) to sprint through the perimeter
- Any player with SP 11+ armor draws looks — too military for a crowd of office workers

**Getting Clear:**
- They need to get at least 3 blocks away before the perimeter locks down
- Options: jack a car (TECH DV 15), grab a cab (STREETWISE DV 11 to find one willing), disappear into the metro (STEALTH DV 13)
- Once clear, they need to decide where to go. Fredrik's fixer instincts kick in — he knows someone who can help. He knows who gave them this job in the first place.

**GM Note:** Keep this fast. 10-15 minutes of play. Don't let it become a full combat encounter. The point is chaos, adrenaline, and getting out. Adventure 1 ended with a big fight — this needs to open with momentum, not another slog.

### Part 2: Kojo

**Who is Kojo Asante?**

Fredrik's established fixer contact. A middle-tier operator who brokers merc work across the Combat Zone and lower corpo districts. He's the one who brought the original job to the players — "simple gig, install some new cyberware, do a run, good money." He sourced it from a cutout and didn't ask too many questions because the money was right.

He's been watching the news. He knows what happened. He's been expecting a call.

**The Meeting:**

Kojo suggests meeting at **Suki's** — a ramen joint in a basement under a laundromat in Japantown. No cameras, no network access, Faraday mesh in the walls (Kojo had it installed). The owner owes him.

- Kojo is visibly rattled. He's normally smooth — now he's chain-smoking and checking the door
- He opens with: *"Before you say anything — I didn't know. I swear on my mother's chrome I didn't know."*

**What Kojo knows:**
- The job came through a cutout called **Slide** — a data broker who moves gig postings between fixers and clients. Slide is now unreachable (probably dead or in hiding)
- The cyberware installation was part of the gig package — "client provides the chrome, you provide the bodies." Not unusual for sponsored runs
- The "Road Runner" implant was presented as a mobility booster. Nothing flagged
- Since the news broke, Kojo has been digging. He's confirmed the client trail leads back to an Arasaka shell company
- He's heard whispers of an Arasaka internal shitstorm — something went very wrong on level 17 (the players)

**What Kojo can do for them:**
- Provide a safehouse for 24 hours max — a shipping container in the docks, converted, clean. *"After that you're too hot for me to hold"*
- Connect them with the Mole, who reached out to Kojo through an anonymous dead-drop system after Adventure 1. Kojo has a one-way contact protocol
- Point them toward **Dr. Yuki Voss** — the ripperdoc. *"If anyone can get Arasaka iron out of your skull without killing you, it's Voss. But she doesn't work for free and she doesn't work for strangers."* Digitz knows her (character background), which helps
- Give them street intel: Arasaka has people on the ground, not just corporate security. A dedicated team. *"These aren't badge-flashers. These are the kind of people that make other people disappear."*

**HUMAN PERCEPTION / CONVERSATION checks:**
- DV 13: Kojo is telling the truth about not knowing
- DV 15: He's also scared for himself — if Arasaka connects him to this, he's dead
- DV 17: He's already thinking about how to profit from this somehow, once the heat dies down. Old habits.

**Kojo's Stash — The Ricochet:**

Before they leave, Kojo opens a locked shipping container behind the laundromat. Inside, under tarps and crates of miscellaneous merc gear, is a military surplus weapon system he's been unable to move.

*"I picked this up six months ago off a Militech logistics convoy that had an 'accident.' Haven't been able to sell it — nobody in this city wants a defensive emplacement. Everyone's mobile, everyone's on the move. But you... you're about to do the one thing nobody does in Night City. You're going to sit still and wait."*

**Ricochet Mk.IV — Fully Automatic Counter-Battery System**

A portable anti-sniper suppression platform. Military hardware, designed for fixed-position defense against ranged attacks.

**Components (3 pieces, must all be deployed):**
- **2x Radar Units** — Tripod-mounted millimeter-wave sensors, each about the size of a briefcase. Placed to the left and right of the defended position, ideally 15-25 meters apart. They triangulate incoming fire by tracking projectile trajectories in real-time
- **1x Turret Unit** — A drum-fed automatic weapon on a swivel mount. Feeds targeting data from the radar units. When incoming fire is detected within its coverage zone, it calculates the origin point and returns fully automatic suppressive fire

**Stats:**
| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Damage | 4d6 (fully automatic burst) |
| ROF | 3 (fires up to 3 bursts per round in response to incoming fire) |
| Range | 200m effective |
| Coverage Arc | 120 degrees (determined by radar unit placement) |
| Ammo | 500 rounds drum (roughly 30 bursts before reload) |
| SP | Turret housing SP 15, Radar units SP 8 |

**Deployment Rules:**
- Setup takes **10 minutes** with TECH DV 13 (or 20 minutes without a check). All three components must be placed and linked
- Radar units must have line of sight to the engagement zone and to each other
- The turret fires *reactively* — it does NOT fire first. It only engages when it detects incoming projectile trajectories within its arc. Someone has to shoot at the defended position first
- Fires at the end of any round in which an enemy fires into the coverage zone. Targets the origin point of the most recent incoming fire
- Can be fooled: throwing objects, decoy shots from an unexpected angle, or flanking outside the radar arc
- EMP will knock out the radar units (not hardened). Without radar, the turret is just a dumb gun
- If a radar unit is destroyed, coverage narrows and accuracy drops: -4 to hit with only one radar unit active

**Tactical Implications:**
This is a game-changer for the clinic defense — but only if the players plan ahead. They need to:
1. Scout the clinic's surroundings and choose placement for the radar units
2. Set up the turret with a clear firing arc toward the most likely approach
3. Bait Reyes' snipers into firing so the Ricochet can return fire

The Ricochet is devastating against snipers and fixed positions. It's less effective against breach teams who close fast and enter the building (inside the radar arc = no coverage). Smart players will use it to neutralize the ranged threat while they handle CQB themselves.

**GM Note:** This is a powerful piece of kit that rewards preparation. If the players set it up well, it should feel earned — they got it from Kojo, they transported it, they deployed it. If they don't set it up (or set it up poorly), that's on them. Don't remind them. Let player agency drive it. Also note: Reyes is smart. After the first burst, he'll figure out what it is and adapt — pull snipers back, try to flank outside the arc, or send someone to disable a radar unit.

**Fredrik's Operator Ability:**
This is a natural moment for Fredrik's Fixer role to shine. He can use Operator to:
- Get better terms on the safehouse (48 hours instead of 24)
- Extract more detailed intel from Kojo about the Arasaka team's movements
- Negotiate Kojo's ongoing support (supplies, transport, backup comms)

### Part 3: The Mole's Warning

The Mole makes contact through Kojo's dead-drop protocol — an encrypted data chip left in a specific vending machine. Digitz decrypts it.

**The message (audio, distorted voice):**

*"You did well. The primary transmitter is destroyed. But Lazard has a mobile backup unit — military spec, vehicle-mounted, lower range. He's pulled it from a black site storage facility. His recovery team is already in the city.*

*The backup needs to calibrate to your specific implant signatures. That takes time — maybe 18 to 20 hours from when they activated it. After that, they just need to get within about 2 kilometers and they can reactivate the droneware.*

*You will not know it's happening until it's already happened.*

*The droneware has to come out. All of it. But it's threaded through your primary neural interface — a standard ripperdoc will paralyze you or kill you trying. You need someone who understands military-grade Arasaka neural bridging.*

*I can't help you anymore after this. Internal security is tearing the building apart. I need to go dark.*

*Don't waste the time I've bought you."*

**What the players learn:**
- The droneware is still active and can be re-triggered
- There's a mobile backup transmitter being calibrated
- They have roughly 18-20 hours (the players' best estimate)
- Extraction requires a specialist ripperdoc
- The Mole is cutting contact

**GM Note:** This is the moment the adventure's stakes crystallize. The players went from "we won" to "we're still fucked." Let it land. Give them a beat of silence after reading/playing the message.

### Part 4: Dr. Voss

**Who is Dr. Yuki Voss?**

A former Trauma Team combat surgeon who went independent after a falling-out with corporate leadership. She runs a hidden clinic in the old waterfront district — a converted warehouse behind a fish market. The smell keeps people away. The clinic itself is pristine.

Voss is in her 50s, precise, no-nonsense. She has a full surgical suite, military-grade tools, and the skills to match. She's one of maybe three people in Night City who could extract Arasaka neural-interface cyberware without killing the patient.

Digitz knows her from a previous job (character background: "friends with a ripperdoc"). She patched him up after a net run went bad. They have a good relationship — but this is a big ask.

**The Clinic:**
- Behind Tanaka's Fish Market, waterfront district
- Entrance through a freezer door (literally walk through hanging fish)
- Inside: clean, bright, well-equipped. Two operating tables, monitoring equipment, shelves of cyberware components
- Voss has a bodyguard — **Udo**, a massive full-borg conversion who sits in the corner reading paperback novels. He doesn't talk much. Base 16, HP 60, SP 18, Cyberarm Hammer Fist 4d6

**Meeting Voss:**

She agrees to see them because of Digitz. She examines the droneware implant (TECH check on one player, takes 20 minutes).

Her assessment:
- *"This is sophisticated work. Arasaka R&D, not commercial. It's wired into your neural link at the brainstem junction. A clean extraction is possible, but it takes about 90 minutes per patient and I need a specific component I don't have."*
- The component: **an Arasaka-manufactured neural bridge decoupler** — a precision tool designed for safely disconnecting military cyberware from neural tissue. Without it, she'd have to cut manually, which has about a 40% chance of permanent nerve damage
- *"Trauma Team medical depots stock them. They handle enough corporate casualties that they keep military-spec surgical tools on hand."*

**The Favor:**

Voss needs the neural bridge decoupler. She knows exactly where one is — the **Northside Trauma Team Medical Depot**, a heavily secured supply warehouse that stocks high-end medical equipment for Trauma Team's premium clients.

She can't go herself — she burned that bridge when she left Trauma Team. But she has an inside contact: **Kira Nantes**, a Trauma Team logistics dispatcher who owes Voss for saving her daughter's life off-the-books. Kira can get them access information, security schedules, and a window — but she won't do more than that.

*"Get me the decoupler. I'll have you on the table within the hour."*

**GM Note:** Voss is not negotiable on this. She genuinely cannot do the surgery safely without the tool. This isn't a shakedown — it's a medical reality. If players try to pressure her, she'll refuse. *"You can threaten me all you want. If I nick your brainstem, you spend the rest of your life drooling into a cup. Your call."*

### Part 5: The Depot Job

**Northside Trauma Team Medical Depot**

A mid-sized warehouse in the industrial Northside district. From the outside it looks like any other warehouse — no Trauma Team branding, no obvious security. That's intentional.

**Intel from Kira Nantes:**

Kira meets them briefly at a parking garage. She's nervous, fast, professional.

- Depot address and layout sketch (hand-drawn on actual paper — no digital trail)
- Security rotation: 2 guards on exterior patrol, 2 inside at the monitoring station, rotating every 4 hours. Shift change at 02:00 — 5-minute window where coverage overlaps and attention drops
- Electronic security: keycard access (she provides a cloned card — works on exterior doors only), interior motion sensors, camera system monitored from the inside station
- The depot is NOT connected to Trauma Team's main network — it runs local. No remote alarm, but the guards can trigger a manual alert that brings an armed AV response in under 4 minutes
- The decoupler is in **Medical Storage Bay C** — high-value surgical tools, locked cabinet. TECH DV 15 to bypass the lock, or just break the glass (triggers local alarm)
- *"You have maybe 15 minutes from entry to exit before something goes wrong. Don't kill anyone. These are my people."*

**Depot Layout:**

```
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │              Loading Dock                  │
    │         (exterior, 2 guards patrol)        │
    ├────────┬─────────────────────┬─────────────┤
    │        │                     │             │
    │ Bay A  │      Bay B          │   Bay C     │
    │ Drugs  │   General Medical   │  Surgical   │
    │ (vault)│   Supplies          │  (locked)   │
    │        │                     │             │
    ├────────┴──────┬──────────────┴─────────────┤
    │               │                            │
    │  Monitoring   │      Staff Area            │
    │  Station      │   (break room, lockers)    │
    │  (2 guards)   │                            │
    └───────────────┴────────────────────────────┘
```

**Approach Options:**

1. **Stealth (recommended window: 02:00 shift change)**
   - Use Kira's cloned keycard to enter through loading dock
   - STEALTH DV 13 to cross the loading dock unseen
   - Interior motion sensors: ELECTRONICS/SECURITY DV 15 to disable, or STEALTH DV 17 to navigate around them
   - Bay C lock: TECH DV 15 or PICK LOCK DV 13
   - Exit clean: if no alarms triggered, they walk out

2. **Fast and loud**
   - Breach loading dock, neutralize exterior guards
   - Interior guards will trigger alert — 4 minutes until AV response
   - Smash Bay C cabinet, grab the decoupler, get out
   - Must be gone before the AV arrives

3. **Social engineering**
   - Fredrik could attempt to bluff entry using the cloned keycard and some convincing corpo attire (he has Businesswear in his loadout)
   - PERSUASION DV 15 to convince monitoring station he's an authorized auditor / inspector
   - If successful, can walk to Bay C with an escort — then needs a distraction to access the cabinet
   - Digitz could provide the distraction via netrunning (trigger a false system alert elsewhere in the building)

**Depot Security:**

| Type | SP | HP | Weapon | Notes |
|------|----|----|--------|-------|
| Exterior Guard (x2) | 11 | 35 | Heavy Pistol 3d6 | Professional, not aggressive. Will call it in before engaging |
| Interior Guard (x2) | 11 | 35 | Heavy Pistol 3d6, Taser | Monitor station, cameras. Alert button within reach |
| AV Response (if alert triggered) | 15 | 45 | Assault Rifle 5d6 | 4-person team, arrives in 4 minutes. Overwhelming force |

**Complications:**

- **The Vault (Bay A):** Contains controlled pharmaceuticals worth 50,000+ EB. Players might be tempted. Taking anything from Bay A guarantees Trauma Team comes after them personally — not just an alert, but a vendetta. Kira also gets burned. Worth mentioning to players who eye it.
- **Guard Dogs:** Two cybered-up guard dogs patrol the interior on a random schedule. AWARENESS DV 13 to hear them coming. Can be distracted with food from the break room or put down (HP 20, no armor, Bite 2d6). Killing the dogs will cause the guards to investigate.
- **Kira's Cloned Card:** Works on exterior doors and Bay B. Does NOT work on Bay C or Bay A — those are higher clearance. But it opens all the doors you need to *get to* Bay C.

**The Decoupler:**
- A precision instrument, about the size of a large pen, in a padded case
- Clearly labeled with Arasaka manufacturing stamps (ironic)
- There are three in the cabinet — taking one won't be immediately noticed during a routine check

**GM Note:** This should play as a heist/infiltration. Reward planning and smart play. The stealth route is very doable if the players coordinate. The loud route works but burns bridges and starts a second clock (Trauma Team investigation). Ideally this scene runs 60-90 minutes of play.

### Part 6: Arasaka Closing In (Interstitial Pressure)

While the players are working the depot job, the Arasaka recovery team is searching.

**Corporal Reyes' Team:**
- 6-person squad in an unmarked van, plus a second vehicle carrying the mobile transmitter (disguised as a delivery truck)
- They're methodical: working from the Arasaka building outward, checking known merc safehouses, bribing informants, scanning for the droneware signal signature

**Pressure events (use as needed between scenes):**

| Event | When to Use | Details |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Street whispers** | After Kojo meeting | Kojo messages them: someone's been asking about them in the Combat Zone. Offering money for sightings. |
| **Signal ping** | After Mole's message, T+8h | Brief painful jolt through all players. Like an electric shock in the back of the skull. Lasts 2 seconds. The transmitter just pinged their implants. It's calibrating. |
| **Ryla Vox calls** | Before or during depot job | Ryla reaches out on the contact they established in Adventure 1. She's been monitoring Arasaka corporate comms and NCPD channels as part of her ongoing investigation into the level 17 story. She's picked up chatter: *"Something's moving. Arasaka has a tactical unit deployed in the city — not standard security, something off-books. And they're not searching for property. They're searching for people."* She doesn't know the details, but she's scared for them and for herself. She asks what's happening. Players can share as much or as little as they want. If they tell her about the droneware extraction plan, she'll want to know where the evidence ends up. *"When this is over — if you have proof — call me. The world needs to see this."* This plants the seed for the epilogue option of giving the droneware to Ryla. |
| **Near miss** | During transit to depot | Players spot an unmarked van moving slowly through an intersection. AWARENESS DV 13: military antennae on the roof, barely concealed. The recovery team, grid-searching. Players need to divert or stay out of sight. |
| **Informant** | After depot job | A street kid approaches one of the players. "Hey, some corpo guy paid me 500 EB to call this number if I saw people matching your description. You want to pay me more to not call it?" |
| **Headaches** | T+12h onward | All players develop persistent headaches. -1 to INT-based checks. The transmitter is narrowing its search. Getting closer. |
| **Vision glitch** | T+16h onward | Brief flashes of heads-up display static across players' vision. Like a system trying to boot up. -1 to AWARENESS checks. Deeply unsettling. |
| **Phantom commands** | T+18h+ | A player's hand twitches involuntarily. Or they take a step in a direction they didn't intend. Momentary, but terrifying. The signal is almost locked. |

**GM Note:** Don't dump all of these. Pick 2-3 that fit the pacing. The goal is mounting dread, not a checklist. The signal pings and physical symptoms are the most effective — they make it personal and physical, not just tactical.

### Part 7: The Extraction (Climax)

The players return to Dr. Voss's clinic with the neural bridge decoupler. She inspects it, nods, and begins prepping.

**The Setup:**
- Extraction takes **90 minutes per patient** — that's 4.5 hours for all three
- One patient on the table at a time. Fully sedated, immobile, helpless
- The other two are free to act
- Voss needs absolute focus during surgery — any disruption risks the patient

**Surgery Order:**
Let the players choose who goes first. This is a meaningful character moment — who trusts whom to watch over them while they're unconscious? The GM should suggest that the most symptomatic player (whoever has the worst signal effects) goes first.

**During the first extraction (90 minutes):**
- Things are tense but quiet
- Voss works methodically. Monitoring equipment beeps steadily
- Players outside can hear the fish market shutting down for the night
- Good time for roleplay between the two waiting players. What are they going to do after this? What does this mean?

**During the second extraction:**

The Arasaka recovery team has triangulated the signal. They're close.

**Alert signs:**
- Udo (Voss's bodyguard) stands up from his chair, puts down his book. He's been watching a feed from exterior cameras. *"We've got company."*
- Exterior camera shows: the unmarked van parked 200 meters up the street. Two figures on a rooftop across from the fish market with optics
- The delivery truck (transmitter vehicle) is pulling into position at the end of the block
- They're setting up. Professional, methodical. They'll breach in about 10 minutes

**The player on the table:**
- 45 minutes into extraction. Voss can't stop now — the neural bridge is partially disconnected. Stopping means permanent damage
- *"I need 45 more minutes. Whatever's happening out there, it is not my problem. Keep them out of my operating room."*

**Reyes' Assault:**

Reyes isn't stupid. He knows what's in there and he's not going to storm a defended position blindly. His approach:

1. **Perimeter first** (minute 0-3): Two snipers on the rooftop across the street. Two operatives cover the back exit (through the fish market). Reyes and one operative at the front
2. **EMP pulse** (minute 3): Targeted EMP to knock out the clinic's cameras and external electronics. Does NOT affect internal medical equipment (it's hardened) or cyberware (too short-range). But the players lose their eyes outside
3. **Breach** (minute 5): Front door breach. Flashbang, then entry

**The transmitter activation attempt:**
At some point during the assault, Reyes activates the mobile transmitter at close range. The **third player** (still with droneware) experiences a full signal breakthrough attempt:
- WILL check DV 17 to resist
- Success: agonizing pain, nose bleeds, but they maintain control. -3 to all actions for 2 rounds
- Failure: **Signal breakthrough event.** Player loses control for 1d6 rounds. They turn on their allies. Full combat stats, no hesitation. After the rounds expire, they snap back — confused, horrified

**This is the nightmare scenario from Adventure 1 made real.** If the player on the table is mid-extraction, the partially disconnected droneware may cause an even more violent reaction (convulsions, Voss fighting to keep them stable).

**The Defense:**

Players need to hold the clinic for 45 minutes. This is a siege defense.

**Reyes' Squad:**

| Type | SP | HP | Weapon | Notes |
|------|----|----|--------|-------|
| Cpl. Reyes | 15 | 50 | Assault Rifle 5d6, Frag Grenades | Smart, tactical. Will pull back and reposition, not charge blindly |
| Sniper (x2) | 11 | 40 | Sniper Rifle 5d6 | Rooftop across street. Can fire into clinic through windows if cover is blown |
| Assault Operative (x2) | 13 | 45 | Heavy SMG 4d6, Flashbang | Breach specialists. One front, one back |
| Tech Operative | 11 | 35 | Heavy Pistol 3d6 | Runs the transmitter, attempts electronic warfare. Priority target if players identify them |

**Clinic Defenses:**
- Udo holds the front. He's a monster in close quarters (SP 18, HP 60, 4d6 hammer fist) but can be overwhelmed
- The fish market provides good cover and choke points — hanging fish, metal counters, freezer units (SP 15)
- Back corridor is narrow — one person can hold it
- Voss has an emergency shuttered blast door for the operating room. SP 30. Takes 2 rounds to close. Once closed, the surgery is protected but the defenders are locked out of the OR
- Fire suppression can be triggered in the fish market: visibility drops to 5m, all ranged attacks -4
- **The Ricochet** (if deployed): If the players set up Kojo's counter-battery system before the assault, it can cover the street-facing approach — the snipers' rooftop and the front breach point. Ideal radar placement is one unit inside the fish market entrance and one at the corner of the building, with the turret covering the street. Once Reyes' snipers open fire, the Ricochet returns automatic suppressive fire at their position (4d6, up to 3 bursts/round). This forces the snipers to relocate or be shredded, which buys the defenders critical time. Reyes will adapt — after 1-2 rounds he'll order flanking outside the arc or send someone to destroy a radar unit (SP 8, exposed). The Ricochet doesn't solve the fight, but it punishes the opening gambit hard and shifts the assault from "methodical siege" to "desperate breach"

**Tactical Considerations:**
- **Kill the transmitter:** If players identify and destroy the delivery truck (or the tech operative controlling it), the signal breakthrough threat ends. But the truck is 200m away and covered by snipers
- **Kill the lights:** The waterfront has minimal street lighting. Shooting out lights gives an advantage to players with cybereyes (low-light)
- **Call for help:** Kojo could send backup, but it'll take 30+ minutes. Too late for the fight, but useful for cleanup
- **Negotiate:** Reyes is a professional, not a zealot. If the fight goes badly for his team, he'll consider pulling back. But Lazard is on comms demanding results — Reyes knows his career (and possibly his life) depends on completing this mission. PERSUASION DV 19 to convince him to walk away

**Resolution Triggers:**
- **Players hold the clinic:** Voss completes the second extraction. The third player goes on the table. If Reyes' team is neutralized or driven off, the third extraction proceeds without further combat
- **Transmitter destroyed:** Even if Reyes' team is still active, destroying the mobile transmitter removes the time pressure. The droneware can't be activated without it. Fight becomes conventional
- **Reyes' team takes heavy casualties (4+ down):** Reyes pulls back. He's not suicidal. Reports failure to Lazard. Players have time to complete all extractions

### Part 8: Aftermath

All three players wake up droneware-free. Voss shows them the extracted implants — small, ugly things threaded with neural filaments. Hard to believe something that size could take over a person.

**Immediate Aftermath:**
- Players are groggy, sore. -2 to all physical actions for 24 hours post-surgery. The Road Runner MA+1 bonus is gone (it was part of the droneware package)
- Udo is cleaning up. Voss is washing her hands. She's tired but satisfied. *"You owe me. And I don't mean money."*
- The extracted droneware is in three sealed containers on the counter

**The Droneware:**
This is now **physical evidence** of an illegal Arasaka black ops program. Three working (if disconnected) droneware units. Potentially the most dangerous and valuable objects in Night City right now. What do they do with them?

**Options (these shape Adventure 3):**

1. **Destroy them.** Safest option. Walk away clean. But Arasaka's program continues and Lazard is still out there. Nothing changes except they're free.

2. **Give them to Ryla Vox.** The journalist from Adventure 1 who they connected with. Physical evidence plus her footage and investigation could blow the story wide open. Arasaka takes massive public damage. But Ryla becomes a target, and so do they — permanently. *"You sure you want to hand a journalist the keys to a war with Arasaka?"*

3. **Sell to Militech.** Fredrik's fixer network (through Kojo) could broker a sale to Arasaka's biggest rival. Huge payout — potentially 100,000+ EB. But Militech gets droneware technology. *The genie is out of the bottle.* The players are richer. The world is worse.

4. **Leverage against Arasaka.** Contact Arasaka directly (not Lazard — above him) and offer to return the droneware and stay silent in exchange for guaranteed safety and Lazard's head. Dangerous play. Requires trusting a megacorp. But it's the only option that might actually end the threat permanently. *"You'd trust the snake to keep its word just because you're holding its tail?"*

5. **Give them to the Mole.** The Mole could use them as leverage for their own extraction from Arasaka, or as evidence in an internal power play to bring Lazard down from the inside. The quietest option, but relies on someone they barely know.

**GM Note:** Don't force a choice immediately. Let the players discuss. This is the epilogue conversation — it should breathe. If they can't decide, that's fine. They take the droneware with them and the decision hangs over Adventure 3.

## Events (Random/Triggered)

### Signal Breakthrough
Can occur any time after T+18h if droneware is still installed.

**Trigger:** GM discretion, or when dramatically appropriate (worst possible moment).

**Effect:** One player (GM choice, ideally the one in the most dangerous position) loses control.
- WILL check DV 17 to resist
- Failure: player is under droneware control for 1d6 rounds
- Controlled player fights with full combat stats, no hesitation
- Other players must subdue (not kill) their friend
- After control fades: confusion, nosebleed, no memory of the episode
- If this happens during Voss's surgery and the affected player is on the table: convulsions, Voss must make SURGERY DV 17 or the patient takes 2d6 direct damage and extraction time extends by 30 minutes

### Lazard's Desperation
If Reyes fails, Lazard escalates.

**Post-assault options (for Adventure 3 setup):**
- Lazard puts a public bounty on the players — frames them as the cyberpsychos from the original news footage. Every bounty hunter in Night City is now looking for them
- Lazard tries to flee Night City with the remaining droneware data. The Mole tips off the players (one last message)
- Arasaka internal security finally catches up to Lazard — but moves to suppress all evidence, including the players
